Our Story

Atlanta Bengali Forum (ABF) established in 2001 is a non-profit cultural organization of the bengalis in Georgia and around the state of Georgia. Our goal is to foster a spirit of family, friendship, diversity and community between its members through social and community activities, sports events, musical gatherings and religious festivals.

During the 90's − particularly late the 90's, a handful of Bengali families from Atlanta metro area were striving for their homely environment out of their nostalgia. They used to meet each other on almost every occasion just to imitate their home environment at West Bengal and Bangladesh by their regular "Adda" which, they learnt out of experience, was not available anywhere else in this area. They all came from different parts of their professional life − businessman to engineer to medical practitioner, but they never created difference among themselves by bringing their professional life to their pure "Adda". After spending couple of years with informal "Adda"s, they thought of celebrating Saraswati Puja in 2001 which gave birth of Atlanta Bengali Forum (ABF).
